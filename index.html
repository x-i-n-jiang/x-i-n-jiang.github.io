Xin Jiang @ USC

Welcome! I obtained my Ph.D. in Economics from UC Santa Barbara, 2024. I am a Postdoc Teaching Fellow in Economics at the University of Southern California.

I am leading a teaching lab aimed at enhancing undergraduate instruction by introducing innovative approaches to both micro- and macroeconomic theory courses. Drawing on my research in experimental economics, I plan to incorporate behavioral experiments into the classroom. These experiments will provide students with a hands-on understanding of when and why economic theories may fail, bridging the gap between theoretical concepts and real-world applications.

Publication

  • Fortune and Identity
    with Gary Charness, Economics Letters (2023). [Paper]
    [Slides] [Data] [Variables] [R code]

    While group identity can generate in-group bias, the topic of how activities generate group affiliation is largely unexplored. We experimentally study the effect of shared experience on group affiliation, varying shared experiences by paying subjects differently for the same task. The results show that shared fortune leads to in-group bias, while shared misfortune does not.
